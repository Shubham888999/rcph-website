About Rotaract Club of Pune Heritage

Chartered in 2015, the Rotaract Club of Pune Heritage was sponsored by the Rotary Club of Pune Heritage, RID 3131.

Our Charter President, Rtr. Parth Jaokar, exemplified leadership by serving as the District Rotaract Representative (DRR) of RI District 3131 for the Rotary Year 2021-22.

Since inception, the club has been known for its strong leadership, impactful service, and vibrant fellowship.

About the Club Logo

The Rotaract Club of Pune Heritage logo features a Marathi Tilak Pagdi in red, a symbol of Pune’s rich history and cultural pride. Worn by Bal Gangadhar Tilak, one of India’s greatest freedom fighters, the Tilak Pagdi represents leadership, resilience, and a deep connection to heritage.

By incorporating this iconic element, the logo reflects the club’s dedication to honoring Pune’s legacy while fostering unity, service, and community growth.

About This Year’s Theme

CREATE, CONNECT, CONTRIBUTE.

CREATE – This stands for innovation, leadership, and fresh ideas. As Rotaractors, we are creators of change — building impactful projects, nurturing team spirit, and shaping a better future through creativity and purpose.

CONNECT – At the heart of Rotaract lies strong connections. This means bonding with fellow club members, collaborating with other clubs, forming ties with our Rotary family, and engaging with the community. Every connection strengthens our impact.

CONTRIBUTE – Our ultimate goal is to give back — to society, to our club, and to ourselves. Whether it’s time, effort, knowledge, or service, every contribution matters. Together, we make a difference.

About Rotary

Rotary is a global network of 1.4 million passionate individuals from over 200 countries committed to creating lasting change in their communities and around the world. Founded in 1905, Rotary focuses on key areas like peacebuilding, disease prevention, clean water, education, and community development.

About Rotaract

Rotaract is the youth wing of Rotary for individuals aged 18 and above. It empowers young leaders to take action through service, professional development, and fellowship. Rotaract clubs are either community or university-based and work closely with local Rotary clubs.
